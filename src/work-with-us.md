# Work with Us

## How We Can Help

If your company manufactures, maintains, or inspects engineering components, surface defects can affect performance, durability, and safety. Our team at King's College London can help you identify and understand these defects using advanced electron microscopy techniques.

We offer a collaborative assessment service that applies our research expertise to real-world engineering problems. Whether you need to investigate a specific failure, assess a new manufacturing process, or characterise the surface quality of your components, we can provide detailed analysis at the micro- and nanometre scale.

## The Engagement Process

### 1. Initial Consultation

Contact us using the email address below. Tell us about your component, the material, the defect or feature you want investigated, and any specific questions you need answered. There is no obligation at this stage — we will assess whether electron microscopy is the right approach for your needs.

### 2. Feasibility Assessment

We will review your request and discuss what is technically achievable. Some components and defect types are better suited to electron microscopy than others. If our method is not the best fit, we will tell you and, where possible, suggest alternative approaches.

### 3. Sample Preparation and Analysis

If you proceed, you will provide sample material from your component. Our team will prepare the samples and carry out the microscopy analysis using our SEM, TEM, and EDS facilities. The exact techniques used will depend on the specific questions you need answered.

### 4. Results and Reporting

We will provide you with a detailed report including high-resolution images, compositional data (where applicable), and our interpretation of the findings. We are happy to discuss the results with your team and answer follow-up questions.

## What We Can Assess

- Surface cracks and fatigue damage
- Corrosion pits and stress corrosion cracking
- Inclusions and compositional anomalies
- Surface roughness and finish quality
- Microstructural features at grain boundaries
- Wear and erosion damage
